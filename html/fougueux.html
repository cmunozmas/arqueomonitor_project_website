Fougueux wreck

The Fougueux (Temeraire-class) was 74-gun 2nd rate ship of the line of the French Navy, built in 1775 in Lourient's shipyard that took part in the naval Battle of Trafalgar (1805). It was sunk in Las Morenas site at a distance of 1.7 km from the coastline, seaward of the Sancti Petri sand spit, during the violent storm that struck the coast of Cadiz after the battle, which drove the vessel onto a rocky shoal.

A total of 31 guns, a large anchor and part of the hull timbers are preserved at the site.
