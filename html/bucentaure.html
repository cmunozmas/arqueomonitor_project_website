Bucentaure wreck

The shipwreck, located in "Bajo del Chapitel" (Cadiz Bay), is associated with the S.M.I Bucentaure (Tonnant class), a 80 gun 2nd rate ship of the French Navy, of the line built in 1804 in Toulon's shipyard.

It sank on October the 23, 1805 because of a lack of governance and sail due to a strong storm that sent in straight to the reefs located in front of Cadiz city in an average depth of 11 meters.

This wreck currently has 22 iron cannons of different calibers, remains of an anchor, as well as numerous metallic parts and structural components typical from the period between the second half of the 18th Century and first half of the 19th Century, settled in a rocky bottom. Most of the components are exposed and subjected to little energy hydrodynamic conditions due to the shelter provided by their location, within the outer jacket of Cadiz Bay.
